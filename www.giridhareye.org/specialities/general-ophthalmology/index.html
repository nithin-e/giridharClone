General Ophthalmic Services

The General Ophthalmology department at Giridhar Eye Institute offers comprehensive eye care services, including thorough vision checks, spectacle prescriptions, intraocular pressure measurements, and retinal examinations. The department provides specialized screening for diabetic retinopathy and manages complex surgeries for eye injuries. It also offers squint correction and addresses computer vision syndrome through dedicated clinics. Our goal is to deliver complete eye health assessments and effective treatments for various common and complex eye conditions.

Frequently Asked Questions

Explore essential questions about eye health, common conditions, prevention tips, and treatment options to enhance your understanding of Ophthalmology.

How often should I get eye exams?

Regular eye exams are essential for maintaining eye health. Adults should have exams every one to two years, while children may need them more frequently.

What are the common symptoms of eye problems?

Symptoms may include blurred vision, double vision, eye pain, redness, and sudden vision changes. Report these to an ophthalmologist promptly.

How can I protect my eyesight?

Protect your eyesight by wearing sunglasses, eating a balanced diet rich in vitamins, and avoiding smoking.

What treatments are available for dry eyes?

Treatments include artificial tears, prescription medications, punctal plugs, and lifestyle changes to reduce environmental factors causing dryness.

What should I know about eye allergies?

Eye allergies can cause itching, redness, and watering. Avoid allergens and consider antihistamine drops or medications for relief.

How do I manage computer vision syndrome?

Manage it by following the 20-20-20 rule: every 20 minutes, look at something 20 feet away for 20 seconds.

What causes sudden vision loss?

Sudden vision loss can result from various conditions, including retinal detachment, stroke, or severe eye injury. Seek immediate medical attention.

How does aging affect vision?

Aging can lead to conditions like presbyopia, cataracts, and macular degeneration, affecting vision clarity and quality. Regular eye check-ups are vital.
